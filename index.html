Overview

Biotechnology, Bioinformatics, Genomics, Metagenomics, Cowpea viruses and Circadian Clocks currently, the major research focus of my laboratory is the impact of discharged textile effluents, and coal discards on the microbial communities that are associated with the receiving environments for bioremediation purposes. Related works is centered on the genomics of selected archaea, bacteria and fingi isolated from these enironments that could be harnessed for developing bioconversion technologies.

We have collaboration with the Howard Hughes Medical Institute Science Education Alliance - Phage Hunters Advancing Genomics and Evolutionary Science (HHMI SEA-PHAGES) programme to discover novel bacteriophages and annotate phage genomes.

In previous studies, I have looked at the effect of viruses on popular African staple food, Vigna unquiculota (Cowpea) and circadian clocks, with the specific aim of uncovering of new slave oscillator in orange bread mould, Neurospora crassa.
